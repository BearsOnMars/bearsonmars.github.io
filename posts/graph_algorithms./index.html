Graphs and Network Modeling

Using the graph approach to analysis, you consider that the world is connected and, therefore, the entities are often not independent. Information about network leaders - or key nodes - can help find the most efficient way to spread information. Finding the most critical nodes can help disrupt or upgrade the resilience of the network.

  1. What is a graph?
    • used more in mathematical terminology and refers to a general language for describing and analysing entities and their relations or interactions
  2. What is a network?
    • a real-world dataset containing entities and their relationships, such as a social network, a communication network, or a road network.

Problems suitable for Network Analysis

Below are the scenarios uniquely suitable for graph-based analysis:

  1. Self-referencing relationship among entities:
    • A single entity type, and multiple relationships between them
    • a small friendship network has many data joints (called relationships) and users (called nodes)
    • Following use-cases can be solved using graph analysis in this scenario:
      • Detecting network influencers
      • Analysing consequences of vulnerabities in a dependency network
      • Inspecting organisational hierarchies
  2. Transportation network:
    • Allows to optimise route selection based on time, distance or cost
    • Network itself could be analysed as a whole and traffic congestions could be predicted, or critical connections can be found which could disrupt the whole network in case of an emergency, such as an accident
    • Following use-cases are solvable in the transportation network scenario:
      • Logistics and routing
      • Infrastructure management
      • finding optimal paths to make new contacts
